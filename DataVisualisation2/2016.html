GDP of Each Country

The bar chart above displays the GDP (Gross Domestic Product) for each country. Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is a key economic metric that quantifies the total market value of all goods and services produced within a country and is used to evaluate its economic performance. We categorize countries based on their Happiness Scores, and you can filter the display to show countries within a specific Happiness Scores range by selecting that range.

Freedom to make life choice of Each Country

The bar chart above displays the Freedom to make life choice for each country. "Freedom to make life choices" signifies the liberty for individuals to decide and pursue their personal, professional, and social preferences without significant external restrictions, contributing to personal autonomy and well-being. Happiness Score, GDP, Freedom to Make Life Choices, and Life Expectancy are all key indicators used to assess the overall well-being and quality of life in a country. The Happiness Score reflects people's overall satisfaction with life, GDP represents a country's level of economic prosperity, while Freedom to Make Life Choices measures the degree of individual freedom to make personal decisions and pursue happiness. Additionally, Life Expectancy measures the number of years people can expect to live in a healthy state. These indicators together paint a picture of the well-being and quality of life of a country's residents, making them essential for evaluating national policies and societal conditions.
